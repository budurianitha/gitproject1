Sexual contact

Most people diagnosed with HIV in the UK acquire the virus through unprotected vaginal or anal sex. It may also be possible to catch HIV through unprotected oral sex, but the risk is much lower.

The risk is higher if:

1.people with a current or previous partner with HIV

2.people with a current or previous partner who is from an area with high HIV rates

3.people who are from an area with high HIV rates

4.people who engage in chemsex (using drugs to help or enhance sex)

5.men who have unprotected sex with men

6.women who have unprotected sex with men who have sex with men

7.people who inject drugs and share equipment

8.people who have unprotected sex with somebody who has injected drugs and shared equipment

9.people who share sex toys with someone infected with HIV

10.people with a history of sexually transmitted infections, hepatitis B or hepatitis C

11.people who have had multiple sexual partners

12.people who have been raped
